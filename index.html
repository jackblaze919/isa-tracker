Monday Workout A

1. Backpack hip thrust
Wk 1–2: 2×10–15 · Wk 3–12: 3×8–15
  • Upper back against a sturdy sofa
  • Backpack across your hips
  • Ribs down, chin slightly tucked
  • Squeeze 1 sec at the top
  • Don't finish by arching your lower back
2. Supported long-stride reverse lunge
2×8–12 per leg
  • Hold a wall or chair for balance
  • Step back far enough to keep the front heel planted
  • Lean forward slightly
  • Push through the whole front foot to stand
3. Side-lying leg raise
2×15–25 per side
  • Don't let your body roll backward
  • Point the toe slightly toward the floor
  • Raise under control — no swinging

Wednesday Workout B

1. Backpack Romanian deadlift
Wk 1–2: 2×10–15 · Wk 3–12: 3×8–15
  • Hold the backpack close to your legs
  • Small bend in the knees
  • Push your hips backward
  • Keep your back neutral
  • Stand by driving hips forward + squeezing
2. Step-up
2×8–12 per leg
  • Use a completely stable platform
  • Whole working foot on it; push through that foot
  • No jumping or pushing hard off the back leg
  • Step down slowly

💗 How Hard Each Set Should Feel

At the end of a set you should feel you could do 1–2 more clean reps — but not 5 or 6 more.

⏳ Rest

  • 90–150 sec after hip thrusts, RDLs, lunges, step-ups
  • 60–90 sec after side leg raises
Don't rush the rest periods, babe. 🩷

📈 Progression Rule

Write down the backpack weight and every rep. When you hit the top of the rep range on every set for two workouts:

  • Add another book or water bottle to the backpack
  • Drop back to the lower end of the rep range
  • Build the reps back up again
Example: Wk1 15 lb ×10,10 → Wk2 15 lb ×13,12 → Wk3 15 lb ×15,15 → Next: 20 lb ×9,8

🌟 If it gets too easy

  • Slow the lowering to 3 seconds
  • Pause longer at the hardest position
  • Progress to a single-leg version
  • Eventually get stronger bands, dumbbells or gym access

👟 Walking Plan

Don't jump straight to 10,000 steps.

  • Track your normal steps for 3 days
  • Calculate the average
  • First target = average + 1,000 daily steps
  • Add + 500 steps per day each week
  • Build toward 7,000–8,000 daily steps
  • Only go higher if energy, appetite, joints & strength stay good
Most walking should be a pace where you can still hold a conversation. 10,000 is optional, not magic. 🌸

    • Avg weight drops > 0.5 lb/week for 2 weeks straight

    • Weekly average weight rises > 0.5 lb/week for 3 weeks straight, and
