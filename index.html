👟 Walking Plan

Don't jump straight to 10,000 steps.

  • Track your normal steps for 3 days
  • Calculate the average
  • First target = average + 1,000 daily steps
  • Add + 500 steps per day each week
  • Build toward 7,000–8,000 daily steps
  • Only go higher if energy, appetite, joints & strength stay good
Most walking should be a pace where you can still hold a conversation. 10,000 is optional, not magic. 🌸

    🌷 First 14 Days

    Run the plan unchanged for 14 days. Then adjust using the rules below — not emotion or one weigh-in.

    💚 Keep everything the same when

    • Weekly average weight is stable or slowly declining
    • Strength is improving
    • Clothes fit the same or slightly looser
    • Progress photos look stable or improved

    ➕ Add 100–150 cal/day when

    • Avg weight drops > 0.5 lb/week for 2 weeks straight
    • Strength starts declining
    • Energy & recovery are consistently poor

    Add one more: ½ cup mashed potatoes · ½ plantain · 1 cup milk · ½ cup rice · ½ cup beans

    ➖ Reduce 100–150 cal/day only when

    • Weekly average weight rises > 0.5 lb/week for 3 weeks straight, and
    • Clothes are clearly tighter around the waist, or photos show obvious unwanted fat gain

    Cut unmeasured oil/butter/sauce or a small starch portion. Do not cut chicken or protein first.

    🔎 If strength stops improving, check first

    • Were all the workouts done?
    • Was the backpack made heavier?
    • Were reps recorded?
    • Was protein close to 120 g?
    • Was sleep at least 7 hours?
    • Were the sets actually challenging?
